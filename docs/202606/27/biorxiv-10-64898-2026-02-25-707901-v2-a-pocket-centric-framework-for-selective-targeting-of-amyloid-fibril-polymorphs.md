## 摘要
高分辨率冷冻电镜淀粉样纤维结构的快速扩展已经改变了我们对纤维多态性的理解，但在蛋白质选择性或多态特异性淀粉样配体的理性开发方面尚未取得相应进展。一种可能的解释是，配体选择性不仅受全局纤维折叠的调控，还受小分子可及的局部表面口袋的影响。在此，我们对β-淀粉样蛋白、tau蛋白和α-突触核蛋白纤维的400个冷冻电镜结构进行了系统分析。使用统一的相似性指数和最小生成树表示，我们构建了淀粉样结合口袋空间的全局和蛋白质特异性图表示，并研究了表面空腔如何在蛋白质、多态性和结构背景下分布。我们发现许多可检测的口袋在多个纤维折叠中是共享的，并且在某些情况下，在形成淀粉样蛋白的不同蛋白质之间也是共享的，这表明口袋层面的趋同可能导致了淀粉样靶向配体的有限选择性。相反，只有有限的口袋子集占据了口袋相似性空间的孤立区域，这定义了蛋白质选择性或多态限制性靶向的罕见结构机会。对提取纤维结构的进一步分析表明，疾病来源的纤维口袋并不构成完全孤立的口袋组子集，而可能类似于在选定的体外多态性中观察到的口袋。总之，这些结果将淀粉样配体开发重新定义为在受限纤维景观中的口袋层面可区分性问题，并提供了一个结构框架，用于识别有前景的结合位点，同时避免本质上无区分能力的口袋。

## Abstract
The rapid expansion of high-resolution cryo-EM structures of amyloid fibrils has transformed our understanding of fibril polymorphism, yet it has not been matched by comparable progress in the rational development of protein-selective or polymorph-specific amyloid ligands. One possible explanation is that ligand selectivity is governed not only by global fibril folds, but also by local surface pockets accessible to small molecules. Here, we present a systematic analysis of 400 cryo-EM structures of amyloid-{beta}, tau, and -synuclein fibrils. Using a unified pocket similarity index and minimum spanning tree representations, we construct global and protein-specific graph representations of the amyloid binding pocket space, and examine how surface cavities are distributed across proteins, polymorphs, and structural contexts. We find that many detectable pockets are shared across multiple fibrillar folds and, in several cases, across distinct amyloid-forming proteins, suggesting that pocket-level convergence may contribute to the limited selectivity of amyloid-directed ligands. Conversely, only a restricted subset of pockets occupies isolated regions of pocket similarity space, defining rare structural opportunities for protein-selective or polymorph-restricted targeting. Analysis of structures of extracted fibrils further shows that disease-derived fibril pockets do not form a completely isolated pocketome subset, but can resemble pockets observed in selected in vitro polymorphs. Together, these results reframe amyloid ligand development as a problem of pocket-level discriminability within a constrained fibril landscape, and provide a structural framework for identifying promising binding sites while avoiding intrinsically non-discriminatory pockets.
