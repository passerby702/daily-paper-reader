Abstract —Sequence-based deep learning offers a scalable alternative to structure-based scoring for protein–ligand binding affinity prediction, but its progress is difficult to interpret when architectural priors are evaluated only on canonical PDBbind-style splits that leak protein–ligand similarity classes across folds. We present H ONEST AFFINITY , a compact 1D-input affinity predictor used to isolate two common priors under a leak-aware protocol: frozen ESM-2 (650M) per-residue protein embeddings and a learned binary pocket-position marker. The same multi-scale convolutional / single-layer Transformer template is evaluated in three variants: H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET (ESM-2 + pocket marker), H ONEST AFFINITY -N OPOCKET (ESM-2 only), and H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -N OESM (21-vocabulary residue embedding + pocket marker). All three train on 11,513 LP-PDBBind complexes in ∼3 GPU-hours on a single Tesla V100. We benchmark them against five reproduced baselines on the LP-PDBBind 3-tier no-leak hold-out, CASF-2016, and a 161-complex CASF-2016 non-train subset, reporting every method as mean ±std over three random seeds. The central finding is not a uniformly best prior, but a split-conditioned reversal : H ONEST AFFINITY -POCKET achieves the best mean Pearson R on validation, CASF-2016, and CASF-2016 non-train, whereas H ONEST AFFINITY -POCKET -N OESM achieves the best mean Pearson R on every strict LP no-leak tier ( test_cl1 –cl3 ), including relative to our ESM-equipped variants. Thus, both the pocket marker and the 1280-dimensional ESM-2 input help on familiar/CASF-style splits but reduce Pearson R on strict no-leak tiers. We argue that binding-affinity models should report paired canonical and leak-proof ablations, and that deployment-regime-matched variants describe such reversals more faithfully than a single global default. An anonymous code snapshot and preprocessing scripts are linked in the first-page footnote; pretrained checkpoints will be released upon acceptance. 

Index Terms —Protein–ligand affinity, drug discovery, protein language model, ESM-2, leak-proof evaluation, LP-PDBBind, CASF-2016. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Estimating the binding affinity of a small-molecule ligand to a target protein is a central task in early-stage AI-driven drug discovery (AIDD): a fast, accurate scoring function turns a virtual library of millions of compounds into a tractable ranked shortlist. Two families dominate the literature. Structure-based scorers consume 3D complex geometry (e.g., voxel grids, atomic graphs, equivariant networks) and are accurate when crystal poses or high-quality docks are available. Sequence-based scorers consume only the protein amino-acid sequence and the ligand SMILES, sidestepping structure availability and enabling library-scale screening for targets without solved structures. Recent sequence-based models have narrowed the apparent gap to structure-based scoring, but the reported progress remains difficult to interpret. One source of uncertainty is the protein representation itself. Models such as DeepDTA [1], DeepDTAF [2], and recent multi-scale matrix-product archi-tectures [3] typically encode amino acids with a learned 21-token embedding, whereas billion-parameter protein language models such as ESM-2 [4], [5] already encode evolutionary co-conservation, secondary-structure cues, and binding-site signatures. Whether this pretrained protein prior improves binding-affinity prediction under strict no-leak evaluation remains unclear. A second source of uncertainty is the evaluation split. The default PDBbind general/refined/core protocol shares protein– ligand similarity classes between train and test, so models that overfit to those classes can still report strong CASF numbers while failing on dissimilar targets. The leak-proof LP-PDBBind [6] reorganization addresses this issue and exposes 30–50% drops for many state-of-the-art scorers, but few sequence-only papers have re-evaluated their models under this regime. This paper addresses both issues by using H ONEST AFFIN -

ITY as a controlled testbed for protein and pocket priors. HONEST AFFINITY is a 1D-input affinity predictor in which the protein branch can consume pre-cached ESM-2 (650M) per-residue embeddings and/or an optional learned binary pocket-position marker. We study three variants of the same architectural template: H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET (ESM-2 plus pocket marker; familiar targets where pocket annotation is reliable), H ONEST AFFINITY -N OPOCKET (ESM-2 only, no pocket annotation; targets with a sequence and a SMILES), and HONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -N OESM (21-vocabulary protein label embedding plus pocket marker; highest mean R in our strict LP no-leak tiers). The rest of the network is deliberately compact: a multi-scale 1D-convolution encoder, a residual block, a single Transformer layer, a mirrored SMILES branch, and a matrix-product compatibility score followed by an MLP head. All three variants train on a single GPU in ∼3 hours.

A. Sequence- and voxel-based affinity models 

DeepDTA [1] introduced parallel CNNs over protein and SMILES character sequences and remains a canonical sequence baseline. Pafnucy [7] discretizes the protein–ligand complex into a 21 3 voxel grid with 19 atom-type channels and applies a 3D CNN. DeepDTAF [2] adds an explicit pocket-sequence channel and concatenates protein/pocket/ligand features into a deeper convolutional pipeline. DEAttentionDTA [8] introduces a dynamic-embedding self-attention layer that re-weights protein tokens by ligand context. CAPLA [9] uses cross-attention over residue–atom features. Multi-scale convolution combined with matrix-product affinity scoring is taken up by recent work [3], one of the sequence-only baselines against which we compare. 

B. Protein language models in molecular tasks 

ESM-2 [4] and its earlier variants [5] have been shown to encode tertiary-structure cues recoverable by linear probes, and have been integrated into single-sequence structure prediction pipelines and into protein–protein and protein–ligand interface predictors. Comparable ligand-side foundation models include Uni-Mol [10] and MolFormer [11]. We inject frozen ESM-2 embeddings into the multi-scale convolutional / matrix-product scoring template that has been successful on CASF-style evaluation, then test whether the resulting protein prior still helps under the LP-PDBBind regime. 

C. Data-quality and leak-proof benchmarks 

The PDBbind database [12], [13] has long anchored protein– ligand affinity benchmarks, but the standard general/refined/core splits permit leakage of similar protein–ligand pairs across folds. The leak-proof LP-PDBBind [6] reorganization clusters the database and exposes three increasingly strict no-leak test tiers; reported drops on this benchmark have been 0.10 –0.20 in Pearson R for several published scorers. We adopt LP-PDBBind as our primary evaluation, complemented by CASF-2016 for comparability with prior work. III. M ETHOD

E. Training and reporting protocol 

We train with mean-squared error on − log K targets using AdamW [15] (lr 10 −4, weight decay 10 −4, ϵ=10 −6), a cosine annealing schedule [16] over 80 epochs (early-stopped with patience 20 on validation RMSE), batch size 32, and gradient clipping at norm 1.0. Training is performed in single precision (fp32) end-to-end. ESM-2 weights are frozen throughout. For reporting, every H ONEST AFFINITY variant, every ablation, and every reproduced baseline is trained from scratch with three in-dependent random seeds; we report the per-split mean ±standard deviation across the three runs and use validation RMSE for model selection within each seed. No single best run is hand-picked. All experiments run on a single NVIDIA Tesla V100-DGXS GPU (32 GB HBM2) hosted on an Ubuntu 20.04 DGX Station (4 V100s available; only one card is used per training run); the host has 256 GB system RAM. The codebase is implemented in PyTorch 2.1 with CUDA 11.8. ESM-2 (650M) embeddings are extracted once with the official fair-esm release and cached as compressed NumPy arrays on disk (24 GB for PDBbind v2020R1). One full training run of H ONEST AFFINITY takes approximately 3 hours on this hardware; inference is ∼10 ms per complex once the embedding cache is warm. IV. D ATASETS AND SPLITS 

A. PDBbind v2020R1 

We use the v2020R1 reprocessed release (Aug. 2025), which contains 19,037 protein–ligand complexes. We extract amino-acid and pocket sequences from the supplied protein and pocket PDB files using BioPython [17], with secondary structure assigned by DSSP [18] (used only for the CAPLA-style baseline; H ONEST AFFINITY does not use SSE). Ligand SMILES are obtained via RDKit [19] from the supplied SDF/MOL2 files. After preprocessing, 19,032 complexes have all four streams (protein, pocket, ligand, label) available; the remainder fail due to malformed coordinates or unparseable ligands. Affinity targets are − log Kd/K i/IC 50 as recorded. 

B. LP-PDBBind 3-tier hold-out 

We adopt the leak-proof splits of [6] on the v2020 entries:

C. CASF-2016 and CASF-2016 non-train subset 

We additionally evaluate on CASF-2016 (285 complexes; the standard scoring-power benchmark [13]), which contains some entries also present in LP-PDBBind train . To separate CASF entries seen during LP training from those not used for training, we also report CASF-2016 non-train : the 161 CASF-2016 entries that are not assigned to LP-PDBBind train . This subset is not a fully independent benchmark: in our split file, 42 of the 161 complexes overlap LP validation, 108 appear in at least one LP no-leak test tier, and 11 are outside the LP split table. We therefore use it only as a non-training CASF diagnostic. We do not include CASF-2013 because the canonical PDB list was not bundled in the v2020R1 distribution we used. V. E XPERIMENTS 

A. Baselines 

We re-implement and re-train five competitive baselines on identical LP-PDBBind splits:  

> •

DeepDTA [1] — parallel CNNs over protein and SMILES character sequences.  

> •

Pafnucy [7] — 21 3 voxel grid with 19 atom-type channels, 3D CNN. We regenerated the 19-channel grids for all 19,032 PDBbind v2020R1 complexes.  

> •

DeepDTAF [2] — protein, pocket and ligand convolu-tional towers with concatenation.  

> •

DEAttentionDTA [8] — dynamic embedding plus self-attention re-weighting (Bioinformatics 2024).  

> •

Multi-scale matrix-product [3] — a recent 1D-input multi-scale CNN–Transformer baseline reproduced from the published reference implementation. All baselines use their reference hyper-parameters (no tuning to LP splits), are reproduced in single precision (fp32) on the same single NVIDIA Tesla V100 (32 GB) GPU as H ONEST AFFINITY ,and share our preprocessed data tables for a strictly controlled comparison. Each baseline is trained from scratch with three independent random seeds (matching the H ONEST AFFINITY 

protocol) and reported as mean ±std. 

B. Main comparison 

Tables I–III report Pearson R, RMSE, and concordance index (CI) for the five reproduced baselines and the three HONEST AFFINITY variants. The results are not dominated by a single configuration. On the validation and CASF-style splits, HONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET has the highest mean Pearson R and CI, improving over the closest non-H ONEST AFFINITY 

competitor, DeepDTAF, by 0.017 on validation, 0.020 on CASF-2016, and 0.008 on the CASF-2016 non-train subset. The last margin is small relative to seed variability and should be interpreted as a best-mean observation rather than a substantial improvement. The strict LP-PDBBind tiers show the opposite pattern. HONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -N OESM, which keeps the pocket marker but replaces ESM-2 with a learned 21-vocabulary residue embedding, obtains the highest mean Pearson R on all three no-leak tiers: it ties DeepDTAF on test_cl1 and exceeds it by 0.014 on both test_cl2 and test_cl3 .It also gives the lowest RMSE on test_cl2 /test_cl3 .Meanwhile, H ONEST AFFINITY -N OPOCKET consistently im-proves over H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET on the strict LP tiers. Together, these trends indicate that both added priors–the pocket marker and the frozen PLM representation–can become counterproductive under no-leak evaluation, so the appropriate HONEST AFFINITY variant depends on the deployment regime rather than on a global ranking. Because several margins are small, we treat best-mean rankings as descriptive unless supported by paired resampling. We average predictions across the three seeds for each method and compute paired bootstrap intervals. These inter-vals show that H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -N OESM improves over H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET on all three strict LP tiers (bootstrap ∆R = +0 .037 , +0 .060 , +0 .082 , all intervals > 0

and all RMSE deltas < 0), and improves over DeepDTAF on 

test_cl2 /test_cl3 but not on test_cl1 . The familiar-split gains of H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET over DeepDTAF remain descriptive because the 95% intervals cross zero on validation, CASF-2016, and CASF-2016 non-train. 

C. Ablation: frozen ESM-2 vs. learned residue embeddings 

To isolate the contribution of pretrained protein context, we re-train the H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET architecture with a 21-vocabulary residue embedding replacing the ESM-2 features, a configuration we hereafter call H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -NOESM and treat as a third deployable variant rather than only as a diagnostic ablation (Table IV). The data reveal a second split-conditioned reversal that mirrors the pocket-marker reversal of Section V-D : ESM-2 improves Pearson R on the canonical / familiar splits (val: ∆ = +0 .019 in favor of ESM, CASF-2016: +0 .034 , CASF-2016 non-train: 

+0 .014 ), but reduces Pearson R on every strict LP no-leak tier (−0.024 on test_cl1 , −0.042 on test_cl2 , −0.064 on 

test_cl3 ). Cross-referencing Table I, H ONEST AFFINITY -POCKET -N OESM is the highest-mean-R H ONEST AFFINITY 

variant on every strict LP tier, matching DeepDTAF on cl1 

and exceeding it on cl2 /cl3 . The mechanism we propose is consistent with the Discussion (Section VI): PLM fea-tures primarily encode within-family discriminators, including residue conservation, secondary-structure cues, and binding-site signatures, that are highly informative when the test target’s family overlaps the training distribution but can become uninformative or misleading when LP similarity filtering removes that overlap. The fact that two structurally unrelated components exhibit the same canonical-vs-LP reversal supports our view that this pattern is tied to the evaluation regime rather than to either component alone. D. Ablation: pocket-position marker 

Table V compares the ESM-equipped variants with and without the pocket marker. The marker improves R on the canonical splits where the test target’s protein and pocket geometry are likely represented in training (val, CASF-2016, CASF-2016 non-train), and reduces R on all three strict LP no-leak tiers, where the test pocket is by construction dissimilar from anything in training. We interpret this as the marker providing an inductive bias toward training-distribution pocket geometry; that bias is informative when the test pocket is familiar and can be misleading when it is not. Combined with the parallel ESM reversal of Section V-C , this yields the three deployment-regime-matched recommendations summarized in the introduction: H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET for familiar targets with a pocket annotation, H ONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -NOESM for strict LP-style targets with a pocket annotation, and H ONEST AFFINITY -N OPOCKET for targets without a pocket annotation. The reversal itself would have been concealed under canonical-only or LP-only reporting, which is why we treat paired split evaluation as a methodological requirement rather than a supplementary check.

VII. C ONCLUSION 

We presented H ONEST AFFINITY , a 1D-input protein–ligand affinity predictor that injects frozen ESM-2 (650M) embeddings and an optional pocket-position marker into a compact multi-scale convolution / matrix-product scoring template, trained on 11,513 leak-proof complexes in ∼3 GPU-hours on a single Tesla V100 and reported as mean ±std over three random seeds. Rather than collapse the architecture to a single best configuration, we report three variants of the same template, each matched to a distinct deployment regime: HONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET for familiar / annotated targets, HONEST AFFINITY -P OCKET -N OESM for strict LP-style targets with pocket annotations, and H ONEST AFFINITY -N OPOCKET 

for targets without pocket annotations. The paper’s primary methodological message is that architectural components can change sign between canonical and leak-proof evaluations: in our experiments, both the pocket-position marker and the 1280-dimensional frozen ESM-2 input help in-distribution and reduce Pearson R on every strict LP no-leak tier. Single-regime reporting would have concealed both reversals. These results support leak-proof splits as a default reporting regime, paired canonical / leak-proof ablations as a standard diagnostic for new affinity scorers, and multi-variant reporting when deployment regimes differ.
