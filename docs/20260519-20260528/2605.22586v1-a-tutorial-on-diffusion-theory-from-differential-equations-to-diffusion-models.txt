Abstract 

This tutorial develops diffusion models from the viewpoint of differential equations. We begin with the conditional Gaussian forward process and show that this path admits both an ordinary differential equation (ODE) representation and a stochastic differential equation (SDE) representation. Averaging the conditional process over the data distribution then yields marginalized forward ODE and SDE formulations that transport the data distribution p0 = pdata 

to a Gaussian prior p1 = N (0 , I ). We next derive the corresponding reverse-time dynamics, namely the reverse SDE and the reverse probability-flow ODE, both of which are governed by the marginal score ‚ąá log pt(x). This leads to a training objective for score estimation and shows that the standard noise-prediction objective is equivalent to score matching up to an additive constant independent of the model parameters. We then discuss sampling methods for the learned reverse dynamics, including DPM-Solver, as well as guided sampling through classifier guidance and classifier-free guidance. Finally, we compare DDPM and DDIM with the reverse SDE/ODE framework and show that they share the same training objective, while DDPM sampling corresponds to discrete reverse-SDE sampling and DDIM sampling corresponds to reverse-ODE sampling.

8.5 Relation to standard conditional flow matching 

The previous proposition does not say that the usual diffusion loss is identical to standard conditional flow matching in the sense of [ 10 ]. In standard conditional flow matching, one chooses a probability path and directly regresses the corresponding conditional or marginal velocity field. The chosen path may be a diffusion path, but it may also be a non-diffusive path such as the OT path emphasized in [10]. This tutorial already contains the structural ingredients behind that viewpoint. In Sections 1 and 2 we constructed the conditional Gaussian path pt(x | x0), its conditional velocity field ut(x | x0)

in (10) , and the corresponding marginalized forward dynamics. This conditional-to-marginal construction is exactly the kind of mechanism that flow matching exploits. However, the training objective used in Sections 4 and 5 does not directly regress ut(x | x0) or the reverse-ODE velocity 37 h‚ąó(x, t ). Instead, it regresses the scaled score ŌĶ‚ąó(x, t ) and only afterward converts that score into the reverse-ODE velocity via (89). So the clean conceptual picture is: 

‚ÄĘ reverse SDE = diffusion model; 

‚ÄĘ reverse ODE = flow model; 

‚ÄĘ noise-prediction loss = denoising score matching for the reverse SDE/ODE pair; 

‚ÄĘ the same loss = weighted flow matching for the induced reverse ODE; 

‚ÄĘ standard conditional flow matching differs mainly in that it supervises the velocity directly and can use probability paths beyond diffusion paths.
